This state "kinetically locks" the polymerization reaction.Part B: The Activator Powder (Calcium Hydroxide - Ca(OH)2)The dry powder phase supplies the necessary calcium ions to trigger the C-S-H reaction.Components: Primarily Calcium Hydroxide (Ca(OH)2) mixed with any desired aggregate (e.g., sand, stone dust).Curing Process (Thermal Trigger)The cure is initiated instantaneously upon contact between the supercooled slurry (Part A) and the ambient-temperature powder (Part B).Mixing: The rapid mixing causes thermal transfer.Unlocking: The binder slurry immediately melts and warms up.Reaction: The rise in temperature and the presence of calcium ions trigger rapid polymerization, forming the rigid C-S-H gel.Reaction (Simplified): Soluble Silicates + Calcium Ions + Water + Heat $\rightarrow$ C-S-H Polymer Gelüèó ApplicationsThe resulting C-S-H Polymer Stone is suitable for:Structural componentsHigh-abrasion pavers and tilesZero-VOC flooring and finishesSpecialized materials (e.g., radiation shielding)üìú Public Disclosure and LicenseThis disclosure is intended to immediately establish Prior Art for the KLP formulation and process, making it publicly known and accessible.This material (documentation, formulation, and process) is licensed under the MIT License.This is one of the most permissive licenses available, allowing for commercial use, modification, and distribution.
